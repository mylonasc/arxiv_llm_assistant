Using the power of memes: The Pepper Robot as a communicative facilitator for autistic children (cAESAR2023 workshop) (2308.04884v1)

This paper presents the results of a lab involving a Pepper robot as a communicative facilitator for autistic children. The lab showed that the robot was effective in promoting the development of social and communication skills in the participants. The observations and analyses conducted during the interactions provide insights into how robots can be used to make them more empathetic and engaging for autistic children, providing potential immediate enterprise use cases.

Automated Driving Without Ethics: Meaning, Design and Real-World Implementation (2308.04760v1)

This paper proposes a strategy for automated vehicle decision making that takes into account pre-defined parameters and the Ethical Valence Theory. This approach could be used by enterprises to evaluate the social acceptability of their AVs' decision making, and to ensure that their vehicles are making decisions that are in line with their moral positions.

Behavioural analysis of interaction between individuals and a robot in the window (cAESAR2023 workshop) (2308.04861v1)

This research aims to analyse the behaviour of people interacting with a humanoid robot, Pepper, in a shop window. Through systematic observation, the research team hopes to discover reactions and modes of interaction with the robot. This could be used in enterprise settings to better understand customer behaviour and reactions to robots, potentially leading to improved customer service.

The Social Triad model of Human-Robot Interaction (2308.04581v1)

This paper proposes a Social Triad model for Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) to explore trust as a social construct. The model identifies areas for consideration in co-creating trustworthy robotics, and suggests that a robot's trustworthiness may be related to the user's relationship with the deploying organisation. This could be useful for enterprises looking to build trust with customers through robotics.

Physics-aware differentiable design of magnetically actuated kirigami for shape morphing (2308.05054v1)

This study introduces a differentiable inverse design framework for magnetically actuated kirigami, enabling shape morphing in response to stimuli. The framework combines kinematics and energy models into a constrained optimization, allowing for efficient design of complex kirigami structures that can be remotely controlled to morph into intricate target shapes. Potential enterprise use cases include flexible electronics and minimally invasive surgery, with the claimed benefits of increased efficiency and accuracy.

How Does the Inner Geometry of Soft Actuators Modulate the Dynamic and Hysteretic Response? (2308.04722v1)

This paper presents a technique to improve the dynamic response and reduce hysteresis of soft robots by manipulating their internal geometry. Through FEM and experimental validation, the study demonstrates that this technique can improve dynamic response by up to 112% and reduce hysteresis by 21.5%. This could be used in enterprise applications to improve the performance of soft robots, offering direct benefits for control.

Emotion-Conditioned Text Generation through Automatic Prompt Optimization (2308.04857v1)

This paper presents a cost-effective approach to emotion-conditioned text generation, using an automatic prompt optimization procedure. This method changes the prompt by adding, removing, or replacing tokens, and requires only a text classifier to measure the realization of the conditional variable. The optimized prompts achieved 0.75 macro-average F1, compared to 0.22 macro-average F1 for manually designed seed prompts, providing a potential immediate enterprise use case for cost-effective text generation with emotion-conditioning.

Explainable AI in Orthopedics: Challenges, Opportunities, and Prospects (2308.04696v1)

This paper outlines the challenges and opportunities of using Explainable AI (XAI) in orthopedics. XAI models prioritize transparency and interpretability, allowing clinicians, surgeons, and patients to understand the contributing factors behind any AI-powered predictive or descriptive models. This work emphasizes the need for interdisciplinary collaborations to establish standards and guidelines for the adoption of XAI in orthopedics, which could lead to improved patient outcomes and better decision-making in the field.

A Bipartite Graph is All We Need for Enhancing Emotional Reasoning with Commonsense Knowledge (2308.04811v1)

This paper proposes a Bipartite Heterogeneous Graph (BHG) method for enhancing AI systems' emotional reasoning with commonsense knowledge. The proposed method can be generalized to multi-type and multi-grained knowledge sources, and is more efficient than existing knowledge infusion methods. It can be used in applications such as online opinion mining from social media and empathetic dialogue systems, providing potential immediate enterprise use cases with the claimed benefits of improved emotional reasoning and knowledge infusion.

Desiderata for normative models of synaptic plasticity (2308.04988v1)

This paper reviews the current state of normative models of synaptic plasticity and proposes a set of desiderata to ensure that models are precise, powerful, and testable. It provides two examples of models that meet these criteria and could be used in enterprise applications to improve adaptive behavior and neural learning.
